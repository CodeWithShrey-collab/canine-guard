Safety Interaction Tips

Crucial guidelines for safe interaction with stray dogs in your neighborhood.

✋

The "Tree" Technique

If an aggressive dog approaches, stand still, keep hands at your sides, and look at your feet. Most dogs will lose interest if you don't react.

🚫

No Direct Eye Contact

Stray dogs often view direct eye contact as a challenge or threat. Avert your gaze slightly to appear non-threatening.

🎒

Barrier Strategy

If a dog lunges, use any available object (bag, umbrella, or jacket) as a shield to protect your torso and limbs.

First Aid: Post-Bite Protocol

Take these immediate actions if you or someone else is bitten or scratched.

1

Wash the Wound

Immediately wash the area with soap and running water for at least 15 minutes. This is the most effective way to reduce rabies risk.

2

Apply Antiseptic

After washing, apply an antiseptic to the wound. Do not cover with a tight bandage.

3

Urgent Medical Consultation

Go to a doctor immediately for Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP) vaccinations. Never wait for symptoms to appear.

4

Log Incident Report

Use our Reporting Tool so local NGOs can track the animal and map the risk zone.
